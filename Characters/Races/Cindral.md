### Status:
Scorned, divided, and spiritually conflicted  
 - Once followers of The Forgefather, Cindral are seen as betrayers of that deity and now primarily revere The Pale Bloom.  
- They occupy a precarious social standing, scattered throughout the city but mostly concentrated in their own district.  
- Known for their passion, resilience, and deep religious fervour, they hold tightly to their cultural identity despite widespread suspicion.  
- Cindral fill diverse roles such as artisans, merchants, warriors, and religious devotees.

### Culture: 
- Cindral culture centres on endurance through suffering and redemption through faith in Pale Bloom.  
- Their shorter lifespans encourage them to live fiercely and with purpose, embracing both hardship and community.  
- Traditions emphasize loyalty to The Pale Bloom and personal resilience over old Emberwright worship.  
- Family ties vary but often revolve around shared belief and protecting one another from outside scorn.  
- They cultivate physical skill and craftsmanship as expressions of their enduring spirit.
- Their district a centre for artisanship, religious gatherings, and underground resistance to outside oppression. 

### Relationships with Other Races:
- **Humans** are viewed with a mix of resentment and grudging respect, especially for their social dominance.  
- **Kenku** are tolerated but seen as distant and less spiritually committed.  
- **Murks** earn some respect for their quiet endurance and connection to nature.  
- **Velkhari** are considered reckless and unreliable, though respected for their combat prowess.  
- **Stitched** inspire both pity and curiosity, reminders of survival at great cost.  
- **Drenek** are regarded as strong but lacking the spiritual depth Cindral prize.

### Appearance:
- Cindral possess **obsidian-like skin** that shifts in opacity, from glossy black glass to smoke-clouded grey. Beneath the surface, faint **cracks glow with inner fire**, pulsing in rhythm with their breath or emotion.
- Their **eyes burn with steady flame**, varying in hue from deep ember to pale white-gold, betraying mood and vitality.
- Hair, when present, appears as **ashen strands or faintly glowing filaments**, often braided or bound to avoid catching alight during intense work.
- Though **humanoid in shape**, their movements are deliberate, almost engineered — a reflection of their constructed grace and inner discipline.
- Many wear **heat-resistant fabrics, tempered metal, or volcanic glass adornments**, combining function and reverence for their fiery origin.
- In darkness, a Cindral’s body emits a subtle, ember-like glow along their fractures — a haunting reminder of both their fragility and endurance.

### Traits:
##### Emberlight: 
Your skin glows with a faint fiery crackle, shedding dim light in a 3 meter radius around you.
###### Heat Resistance:
You have resistance to fire damage.
##### Emberstrike: 
When you make a melee or ranged Attack, you can choose to ignite your weapon with ember energy as part of that attack action, adding +1 fire damage to the attack. Alternatively, you can spend a Half Action to empower the fire damage to deal 1d6 fire damage instead. This can be used a number of times equal to your Vitality Attribute per Short or Long rest.
##### Fireforged Endurance:
Gain +1 training in Athletics, Crafting, or Endurance.
