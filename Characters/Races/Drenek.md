# Status
*Enduring, stoic, and undervalued.*
Descended from ancient mountain folk or divine labourers, Drenek are physically formidable and built for endurance. Their coarse, stone- or bark-like skin and uncanny control over their own weight set them apart physically. Often relegated to gruelling labour roles in mines, sewers, and catacombs, they are rarely trusted with leadership despite their durability and stamina. Quiet and grounded, they form tight-knit, oath-bound communities and generally avoid magic unless it serves a practical purpose.
# Culture
Drenek culture emphasises perseverance, duty, and community loyalty above all. Traditions often involve oaths and pacts — both personal and communal — that bind them to mutual support. They value physical skill and practical knowledge, often shunning frivolous pursuits or unnecessary risks.

Death is accepted as a natural part of life, and they honour ancestors through stonework and enduring monuments. Their districts are rugged and utilitarian, centred around quarries, sewer networks, and underground passages. Communities maintain clear hierarchies based on merit and service, viewing magic pragmatically and embracing it only when useful for work or defence.
Drenek see themselves as the city's backbone, performing tasks too arduous or dangerous for others — a quiet pride reflected in their craftsmanship and stoicism. While outwardly restrained, they hold deep reverence for endurance, viewing survival as its own sacred art.

# Relationships with Other Races
Humans view them with neutral respect but rarely engage beyond necessity. Stitched and Velkhari are often regarded with suspicion or wariness due to their unstable or unsettling nature. Kenku and Murks are curious but somewhat distant, as their natures and lifestyles differ sharply. Cindral are regarded as passionate but volatile, a contrast to the Drenek's measured endurance. Among themselves, Drenek maintain strong loyalty, often backing each other in conflicts or city politics.
# Appearance
Drenek resemble broad, earthen humanoids whose skin mimics stone, bark, or packed clay, often patterned by natural fissures and mineral striations. Their eyes glow faintly with mineral tones — amber, jade, or dull silver — reflecting the depths of their subterranean origins. Hair is rare; many instead possess ridges, rocky protrusions, or hardened scales across their head and shoulders. Their movements are deliberate and grounded, each step carrying a sense of weight and permanence. They wear durable, reinforced attire, often inscribed with clan sigils or oaths carved into stone or metal plates.

# Traits
## Stone Resilience
Your stony hide grants natural armour. You gain **+1 Damage Reduction** against physical attacks. This stacks with all other sources of Damage Reduction. Applied before damage reaches Sturdiness.
## Weight Mastery
Your uncanny control over your own weight reduces falling impact. When you would suffer falling damage, you may reduce that damage by **half**. This requires no action to trigger.
## Heavy Impact
If you move at least **3m** and then make a melee attack during the same round, that attack deals **+3 damage**. Applied once per round regardless of how far you moved beyond the minimum.
## Unyielding
You have **Advantage** on Tests to resist being knocked prone or forcibly moved.
## Mountain's Endurance
Gain **+1 training** in one of the following skills of your choice at character creation: Athletics or Endurance.
