### Status:
Dominant, privileged, adaptable
- Control the Nobility, most government positions, and the Royal Mage academies.
- Seen as the most "teachable" and capable of mastering magic, reinforcing institutional dominance.
- Other races often view humans as entitled or imperialist but grudgingly acknowledge their organizational power.

### Culture:
- Family structures follow traditional patterns, emphasizing nuclear and extended family bonds, though customs vary between communities. Worldviews are diverse and shaped by personal experiences and social standing.
- Religion and magic are deeply woven into daily life, with humans often leading major Sanctums and magical institutions, reinforcing their social and political dominance.

### Relationships with Other Races:
- Humans hold an exploitative stance toward other races due to their dominant societal position.
- Individuals, especially among Explorers, Rebels, and certain clergy, may form meaningful friendships and alliances with other groups.
- Humans have a positive view of Kenku and Murks, fostered in part by shared religious ties (e.g., the Blind Shepherd’s following among Kenku).
- They are neutral toward Drenek but tend to distrust or look down on Velkhari, Stitched, and Cindral.
- Most major factions, including the Royal Advisors, Merchant Guild, and Magic Institutions, are human-led.

### Appearance:
- Humans in Solasfall display diverse physical traits, with a wide variety of skin tones, hair colours, and eye colours.
- Clothing, hairstyle, and ornamentation vary based on social standing, occupation, and personal taste.
- They generally have medium builds and average height compared to other races, adaptable to a wide range of roles and environments.
