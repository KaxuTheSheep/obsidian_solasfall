The city of **Solasfall** is home to approximately **275,000 inhabitants**, making it a bustling metropolis by pre-industrial standards. It is large enough to sustain a complex society yet still vulnerable to the hardships of a world without industrial technology.

Within this population, diverse **races** contribute to the city’s culture, economy, and politics. Each race brings unique customs, abilities, and social challenges, resulting in a complex web of alliances, tensions, and coexistence. Interracial friendships and partnerships exist, but systemic inequality remains deeply embedded, influencing every district, guild, and institution.
### Races in Solasfall
Races in Solasfall include:
- **Human** – The most common race, humans are versatile and resilient. They dominate the city’s politics, guilds, and military, often acting as mediators between other races. Known for adaptability, ambition, and ingenuity.
- **Stitched** – Constructed beings, patched together from corpses or magical materials. Often misunderstood and feared, they possess unnatural resilience and unique magical affinities. Many serve as scholars, mercenaries, or enforcers.
- **Velkhari** – A mysterious, long-lived race with pale, reflective skin and large eyes adapted to low light. Gifted in arcane arts and stealth, they are rare in the city but highly sought for magical or espionage tasks.
- **Kenku** – Avian humanoids with a mimicry-based language. Agile, cunning, and often involved in trade, thievery, or covert operations. Social bonds are tight, and they are loyal to their flock above all.
- **Murk** – Amphibious, dark-skinned folk adapted to swamps and waterways. Experts in survival, stealth, and guerrilla tactics. Often employed as scouts, fishermen, or in clandestine roles.
- **Cindral** – Fire-touched humanoids with ember-like eyes and faintly glowing veins. Passionate, volatile, and skilled in pyromancy or smithing. Cultural pride and artistry are highly valued.
- **Drenek** – Large, muscular, reptilian humanoids. Resilient and strong, often seen as laborers, guards, or warriors. They value clan loyalty and honor above personal ambition.
