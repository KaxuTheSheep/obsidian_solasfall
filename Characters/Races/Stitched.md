# Status
*Pitied, feared, and often treated as expendable.*
Stitched lack traditional family structures but form strong bonds primarily with the Pale Bloom Sanctum and the Explorers' Guild. Widely used as tireless labourers or explorers due to their resilience and lack of physical needs. Feared by the public due to prior use as Hollow Shade Cultist cannon fodder during wars with the Knights Orders. Despised by the Verdant Hand for their unnatural stasis and resistance to natural cycles of growth and decay.
# Culture
Identity is fluid in early stages and gradually crystallises around one or more dominant past lives. Emotional expression is muted — most avoid forming more than a handful of close bonds. It is taboo to flaunt recognition or connection with former loved ones; jealousy and grief run deep.

A Stitched's personality often mirrors whichever former life aligns with their current function. Younger Stitched are aloof and confused, flooded by conflicting past-life glimpses. Older ones forge stable identities over time as one dominant mana signature emerges from the composite.
# Relationships with Other Races
Most races regard Stitched with unease or fear, associating them with war trauma and necromantic taboo. They are accepted primarily for their usefulness and quiet endurance. Humans and Cindral tend to view them with mistrust, while the Pale Bloom Sanctum shows them cautious reverence. Velkhari respect their endurance but do not form lasting bonds.
# Appearance
Stitched are clearly artificial, with visible seams, patches, and varying skin tones from their previous lives. Body parts may differ in size, colour, and texture. Older Stitched tend to appear more cohesive and symmetrical as their composite identity stabilises. Eyes often glow faintly, reflecting the residual vitality of past lives. Clothing is practical, adapted for exploration, labour, or stealth, often covering or reinforcing seams.
# Soul
A Stitched soul is not one coherent mana signature but many overlapping ones drawn from the lives stitched into their form. This composite signature is unstable in youth and gradually consolidates as one dominant pattern asserts itself. It is neither fully any of its parts nor something entirely new — it is all of them at once, slowly becoming one.

# Traits
## Patchwork Form
Your stitched-together body can separate into autonomous parts.

- As a Half Action at Speed 13, you may detach one body part (hand, arm, leg, head, eye, etc.).
- Detached parts use standard Walk Travel Rate for movement.
- Detached parts share your Blood and Sturdiness pools and cannot act independently — all actions, movement, and abilities come from your own turn's resources.
- Detached parts retain relevant senses. A head can see and hear. A hand can feel. A removed eye can see. Speaking requires lungs.
- Detached hands can perform the somatic components of spells if directed.
- As a Half Action at Speed 12, you may reattach a detached limb.
- Missing body parts can be replaced with those from another creature. Doing so requires a Long Rest to attune to the new part.
## Unravelling
Once per Long Rest, when you would be reduced to 0 Blood, you instead drop to 1 Blood.
## Rethreading
A Stitched must spend time every 3 days maintaining their physical cohesion — resewing seams, reattaching loosened parts, and reinforcing the mana signature binding their composite soul together. This can be performed during any rest period given sufficient time and materials.

- If a Stitched completes their Rethread on schedule, their maximum Sturdiness is maintained normally.
- Each missed Rethread reduces maximum Sturdiness by 3.
- Maximum Sturdiness is fully restored upon completing a successful Rethread.
- If maximum Sturdiness reaches 0 through missed Rethreads, physical disintegration begins — a narrative event handled by the GM.

> **Hollowdeep Note:** Due to the 7:1 time ratio inside the Hollowdeep, a standard expedition between portal openings spans 7 Hollowdeep days — meaning roughly 2 Rethreads are needed during a typical expedition. A stranded Stitched faces severe deterioration before the next portal opens.
