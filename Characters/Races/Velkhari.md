# Status
*Feared, tolerated, and respected for strength.*
Created by a Hollowdeep parasite that fundamentally alters the host's mana signature, transforming them into something new. Marked by pale skin, shark-like teeth, pupil-less eyes, and long tongues — often mistaken for demons. Tolerated only for their martial usefulness and ability to resonate with Rift energy. Most faithful distrust them, though they are not barred from participating in religion. Their transformations are taboo within city walls, even when offered as a form of rebirth to the old or dying.
# Culture
Velkhari form no more than three meaningful bonds; casual associations are common but trust is rare. The transformation overwrites the old self entirely — no memories or feelings of their past life remain. The mana signature that defined who they were is replaced by something new, something shaped by the parasite's influence.

In their own district they thrive in a competitive sparring culture, maintaining a loose but respected hierarchy based on skill and combat prowess. Their perspective on the Hollowdeep varies: some see it as home, others as a place of growth or challenge.
# Relationships with Other Races
Widely mistrusted, especially by Humans and the faithful. Generally tolerated due to their usefulness and fearsome reputation — people avoid provoking them. Drenek and Murks may respect their power but keep a wide emotional distance. Velkhari treat others case-by-case, forming opinions based on individual conduct rather than race.
# Appearance
Pale, almost ghostly skin with shark-like teeth and long tongues. Pupil-less eyes that reflect Rift energy. Typically wear minimal, practical clothing suited for combat or climbing. Their bodies often show faint Rift markings or scars from transformation.

## Rift Step
Harnessing their connection to the Rift, Velkhari can momentarily tear open a shimmering portal in space.
As a Half Action at Speed 10, create a Rift within line of sight and instantly teleport to it, up to **15m** away. Can be used once per any Rest.
## Spider Climb
You can crawl on vertical surfaces and ceilings. Movement on walls and ceilings uses your **Crawl Travel Rate**.
## Predator's Scent
Your parasite-enhanced physiology grants you a heightened sense of smell. You can detect creatures within 9m by scent alone, even through walls or in total darkness. You cannot be surprised by creatures within this range unless they have suppressed their scent. Heavily obscured or odourless creatures may reduce this range at GM discretion.
## Predator's Bite 
As an attack, you may bite a target within melee range, dealing 1d6 + Might Piercing damage at Speed 12 + Grace. If the attack hits, you restore Blood equal to half the damage dealt, rounded down. You may choose to latch on instead — the target becomes Grappled until they break free or you release. While latched, you deal 1d4 Piercing damage and restore half that amount as Blood automatically at the start of each round without spending an action.
