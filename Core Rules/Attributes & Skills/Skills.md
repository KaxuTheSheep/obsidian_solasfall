Skills are specific areas of training and expertise built upon your natural Attributes. While your Attributes show what you’re generally capable of, Skills represent where you’ve focused your efforts and experience.  
When you attempt an action tied to a particular skill, add both your **Attribute Modifier** (representing raw ability) and your **Skill Bonus** (representing training) to your 2d10 roll. This reflects how specialised knowledge sharpens natural talent.

**Training Levels & Skill Bonus**  
At character creation, you have **4 Training Points** to invest in skills. Each level of training in a skill grants a +1 bonus when using that skill. You can spread training broadly for small bonuses in many skills or specialise for higher bonuses in fewer skills.

| **Training Level** | **Description** | **Skill Bonus** |
| ------------------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| 0                  | Untrained       | +0              |
| 1                  | Trained         | +1              |
| 2                  | Skilled         | +2              |
| 3                  | Expert          | +3              |


You gain initial skill training from your **Race**, **Origin**, **Role**, and **Class**, each granting specific skills that reflect your heritage, background, and chosen path. If you gain training in a skill you already have, increase its training level by +1 up to the Highest linked Attribute.  
You can then improve your skills over time through **story milestones** (moments of significant growth), **unique opportunities** (learning from rare teachers, uncovering ancient knowledge), or other significant events in the narrative.

**Using Skills**  
When the GM calls for a skill Tests:
1. Roll 2d10.
2. Add your Attribute Modifier related to that skill.
3. Add your Skill Bonus (based on training level).
4. Compare the total to the Difficulty Number (DN) set by the GM.
Success means you performed the task with trained expertise; failure means you didn’t.

**Skill List**  
Skills cover niche areas. For example, Arcana is the specific skill of deciphering Magic Symbols while insight is for any other use case.

- **Arcana (Insight)** - Understand magical lore, symbols, and arcane effects.
- **Athletics (Might)** - Perform physical feats like climbing, swimming, running, and jumping.
- **Crafting (Grace or Insight)** - Create, repair, or modify tools, Weapons, and items.
- **Deft Hands (Grace)** - Perform delicate or precise tasks with your fingers, such as fine manipulation.
- **Endurance (Vitality)** - Resist poison, fatigue, disease, and harsh environments.
- **History (Insight)** - Recall knowledge of past events, legends, and cultures.
- **Intimidation (Might or Presence)** - Threaten with physical force or wield authority, reputation, or menace.
- **Intuition (Insight or Resolve)** - Sense motives, detect lies, and anticipate danger.
- **Investigation (Insight)** - Search for clues, interpret evidence, and uncover hidden information.
- **Medicine (Insight)** - Diagnose illness and treat wounds.
- **Negotiation (Presence or Resolve)** - Reach agreements, bargains, and compromises through lie or truth.
- **Perception (Insight)** - The ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses.
- **Performance (Presence)** - Entertain through music, acting, or storytelling.
- **Stealth (Grace or Insight)** - Move quietly and avoid detection, or exploit shadows and distractions.
- **Survival (Insight or Resolve)** - Track creatures, find food, and endure the wilds.
