Ranged attacks allow characters to strike targets at a distance using bows, thrown weapons, or similar arms. They follow the same core attack resolution as melee but interact differently with movement, cover, and defence.

## Attack Roll
Roll **2d10 + Grace** and compare to the target's Evasion.

## Arrow Travel
Ranged weapons have two Speed values:

- **Initiation Speed** — when the attack is launched, same as any other action in the resolution order.
- **Travel Rate** — how far the projectile travels per Speed point after launch, measured in metres per Speed point.

The projectile is in flight during the Resolution Phase. A target that moves behind cover before the projectile arrives is protected. Whether a target reaches cover in time depends on their movement Speed compared to the projectile's arrival Speed.

> **Example:** A shortbow fires at Initiation Speed 16. The arrow travels at 15m per Speed point. A target 60m away is hit at Speed 12 (16 - 4 Speed points). A walking character initiating at Speed 10 begins moving at 2.5 seconds into the round — just after the arrow arrives. At 90m the arrow arrives at Speed 10, simultaneous with the walker's initiation — GM call.

## Range
Each ranged weapon has an optimal range. Within optimal range there is no penalty to the attack roll.

Beyond optimal range, the attack roll and damage suffers **-1 per 20m**. There is no cap — extremely long range shots become increasingly unlikely but never mechanically impossible.

## Minimum Range
If a target is within the weapon's minimum range threshold, the attack roll is made at **Disadvantage**. This mirrors the Close-Quarters penalty for reach weapons — a bow is unwieldy at point blank range.

## Cover
Cover represents physical obstacles between the attacker and target. Foundry will prompt for cover percentage when making a ranged attack roll.

|Cover|Effect|
|---|---|
|Partial (~25–50%)|–2 to attack roll|
|Significant (~75%)|–4 to attack roll|
|Full (100%)|Automatic miss|

On a **Critical Success** against a target in Full Cover, the shot may ricochet or find a gap at the GM's discretion.

## Facing
- Attacking from a target's **Rear Arc** grants **Advantage** on the attack roll.
- **Parry** does not apply against ranged attacks.
- **Block** does not apply unless the target's shield is actively facing the attacker.
- **Dodge** applies normally — it is movement based and direction independent.
## Reactions and Cover

A character may spend their Reaction during the Resolution Phase to move behind cover mid-movement. Whether they reach cover before the projectile arrives depends on their movement Speed and the projectile's travel rate. See Movement Types and Reactions.
