**Block** is a **defensive stance** where you commit your weapon or shield to intercept and absorb incoming attacks from the direction you are facing.

## Declaring Block
- **Block is declared during the Declaration Phase** for the round.
- When declared, you enter a **Blocking Stance** that remains active **until your next turn**, unless ended early.
- **Block does not immediately occur** when declared.  
    The **first time** your Block prevents damage this round, it resolves using its **Block Speed**.

## Block Speed
When Block is actually used (i.e., the moment an attack makes contact):
- Resolve Block at the **Speed of the weapon or shield** being used to Block.
- If blocking unarmed, use your **Unarmed Speed**.
If multiple blocking tools are available (weapon + shield):
- You choose which one determines the **Speed** when the Block is triggered.
If an attack hits your block before its activates properly, you instead use half of your **Block Value**. 

## Determining Block Value
When Block is triggered, roll:
- The **Damage Die** of the weapon or shield used to block
- If a shield is equipped, also roll the **Shield Block Die**
**Block Value = Weapon/Shield Damage Die + Shield Block Die (if any)**
If blocking **unarmed**, roll your **Unarmed Damage Die** instead.

## Effect of Block
For the remainder of the round, while the stance is active:
- **Incoming physical damage from the direction you are facing** is reduced by your **Block Value**.
- Damage that remains after reduction is applied to:
    1. **Sturdiness**, and then
    2. **Blood**
Block does **not** prevent non-physical effects (fear, poison, fire, etc.), unless stated otherwise.

## Facing and Direction
Block only protects the **forward arc**.
- **Attacks from the front** → Block applies normally.
- **Attacks from the side or rear** → Block provides **no damage reduction**.
Your facing is determined by where your token is oriented.

## Movement While Blocking
You may still move up to **half your Walking Distance** while maintaining Block.
However:
- Turning to change your **facing** while Blocking is **allowed**, but must be done **when your Speed position is reached** (not immediately on declaration).

## Ending Block
Block ends if:
- You become **Stunned**, **Paralyzed**, **Prone**, or **Forced to release control** (e.g., disarmed).
- You choose to **drop the stance** at the start of your next turn.
- You declare a different **Defensive Stance** (e.g., Parry).
