****Dodge** is an evasive defensive stance focused on footwork, spacing, and avoiding the line of attack.  
It prevents a hit entirely by repositioning the body — it does **not** reduce damage.

## Declaring Dodge (Full Action)
If you declare **Dodge** during the Declaration Phase:
- Your **Evasion increases by (Grace + Insight)** for the rest of the round.
- You may make **one free reposition up to 2 meters** at any point during the round.
- This reposition **does not provoke reactions** and may **rotate your facing freely** as part of the movement.
This represents anticipating threats and actively maintaining evasive footwork.

## Reactive Dodge (Reaction)
If you **have not declared Dodge**, you may Dodge **when targeted by an attack**, using your **Reaction**:
- Your **Evasion increases by your Grace** **against that attack only**.
- You **do not** gain the free reposition step.
- You may still rotate your facing to match the attacker as part of the Dodge movement.
This represents sudden reactive evasion rather than sustained footwork.

## Applies Against

- Melee Attacks  
- Thrown Weapons  
- Ranged Attacks  
- Physical spell attacks that require an attack roll  

Does **not** apply to:
- Area effects (explosions, cones, clouds)
- Attacks that state **“cannot be dodged”**

## Restrictions

You **cannot Dodge** if:
- Immobilised
- Grappled
- Paralyzed
- Reduced to 0 Sanity (loss of coordination & instinct)

## Facing
- Dodge always allows **free facing adjustment** as part of the motion.
- This is true whether Dodge was declared or reactive.
