****Dodge** is an evasive defensive stance focused on footwork, spacing, and avoiding the line of [[Attacks|attack]].  
It prevents a hit entirely by repositioning the body — it does **not** reduce [[Damage Types|damage]].

## Declaring Dodge (Full [[Actions|Action]])
If you declare **Dodge** during the Declaration Phase:
- Your **[[Evasion]] increases by (Grace + Insight)** for the [[Rest & Recovery|rest]] of the [[Rounds|round]].
- You may make **one free reposition up to 2 meters** at any point during the [[Rounds|round]].
- This reposition **does not provoke [[reactions]]** and may **rotate your [[Facing|facing]] freely** as part of the [[Movement Types|movement]].
This represents anticipating threats and actively maintaining evasive footwork.

## Reactive Dodge (Reaction)
If you **have not declared Dodge**, you may Dodge **when targeted by an [[Attacks|attack]]**, using your **Reaction**:
- Your **[[Evasion]] increases by your Grace** **against that [[Attacks|attack]] only**.
- You **do not** gain the free reposition step.
- You may still rotate your [[Facing|facing]] to match the attacker as part of the Dodge [[Movement Types|movement]].
This represents sudden reactive [[evasion]] rather than sustained footwork.

## Applies Against

- Melee [[Attacks]]  
- Thrown [[Weapons]]  
- [[Ranged Attacks]]  
- Physical spell [[attacks]] that require an [[Attacks|attack]] roll  

Does **not** apply to:
- Area effects (explosions, cones, clouds)
- [[Attacks]] that state **“cannot be dodged”**

## Restrictions

You **cannot Dodge** if:
- Immobilised
- Grappled
- Paralyzed
- Reduced to 0 [[Sanity]] (loss of coordination & instinct)

## [[Facing|Facing]]
- Dodge always allows **free [[Facing|facing]] adjustment** as part of the motion.
- This is true whether Dodge was declared or reactive.
