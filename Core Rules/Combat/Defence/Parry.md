**Parry** is a **defensive stance** focused on **deflecting or redirecting** an incoming attack with precise timing. A successful Parry **negates the attack entirely**, preventing all damage and effects — but **failure is punished**, as a mistimed deflection exposes you to a worse hit.

## Declaring Parry
- **Parry is declared during the Declaration Phase.**
- You enter a **Parrying Stance** which remains active until your next turn, unless ended early.
- Parry does **not** occur immediately.  
    It only triggers when **the first attack this round** targets you.

## Parry Speed
When the first attack is made against you:
- Parry resolves at the **Speed of the weapon (or unarmed strike)** being used to parry.
- If **your Parry Speed is lower** than the attacker’s attack Speed, the attack lands **before** your Parry completes → Parry gains **Disadvantage**.
If you are able to meet or exceed their Speed → proceed to the Parry Test.

## Parry Test (Timing Contest)
Make a **Grace Test**.
- **DN = the attacker’s Attack Test result** (their roll total).
**Outcomes:**

| Result               | Effect                                                                                                                  |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Success**          | The attack is **negated** — you take **no damage** and suffer **no effects**.                                           |
| **Critical Success** | The attack is negated, and you may make **one free attack** against the attacker at the speed of the attack you choose. |
| **Failure**          | You take **full damage**, and the attack's damage die is **stepped up by one** (d6 → d8 → d10 → d12).                   |
Parry only applies to **one attack per round**.

## Requirements to Parry
You may Parry if:
- You are wielding **any melee weapon**, including **two-handed** weapons  
    or
- You are **unarmed**, with at least one arm free to perform the deflection.
You cannot Parry if:
- Stunned, Paralyzed, Prone, Restrained, or otherwise unable to move your arms.
- Your weapon is **not drawn**.

## Facing
Parry relies on **awareness and line of sight**.

|Attack Direction|Parry Result|
|---|---|
|From the **front arc**|Parry functions normally.|
|From the **rear arc**|Your Parry Test is made at **Disadvantage**.|
If using **Defensive Footwork / Facing rules**, turning to face during resolution consumes your **Speed position**, not declaration.

## Ending Parry
Parry ends if:
- You perform a different **Defensive Stance** (e.g., switch to Block).
- Your next turn begins.
- You become Stunned / Restrained / Disarmed.
