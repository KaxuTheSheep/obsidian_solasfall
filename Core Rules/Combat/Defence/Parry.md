**Parry** is a **defensive stance** focused on **deflecting or redirecting** an incoming attack with precise timing. A successful Parry **negates the attack entirely**, preventing all damage and effects — but **failure is punished**, as a mistimed deflection exposes you to a worse hit.

### Declaring Parry
- **Parry is declared during the Declaration Phase.**
- You enter a **Parrying Stance** which remains active until your next turn, unless ended early.
- Parry does **not** occur immediately. It only triggers when **the first attack this round** targets you.

### Parry Speed
When the first attack is made against you:

- Parry resolves at the **Speed of the weapon (or unarmed strike)** being used to parry.
- If **your Parry Speed is lower** than the attacker's Attack Speed, the attack lands **before** your Parry completes → Parry gains **Disadvantage**.

If you meet or exceed their Speed → proceed to the Parry Test.

### Parry Test (Timing Contest)
Make a **Grace Test**.
- **DN = the attacker's Attack Test result** (their roll total).

|Result|Effect|
|---|---|
|**Success**|The attack is **negated** — you take **no damage** and suffer **no effects**. You may choose to either make a **free counter-attack** against the attacker at the Speed of the attack you chose, **or** remain in **Parrying Stance** to attempt a second Parry this round.|
|**Critical Success**|The attack is negated. You **both** make a free counter-attack **and** remain in Parrying Stance for a potential second Parry.|
|**Failure**|You take **full damage**, and the attack's damage die is **stepped up by one** (d6 → d8 → d10 → d12). **All defensive stances end** for the remainder of the round.|

### Successive Parries
If you choose to remain in Parrying Stance after a successful Parry:

- You may attempt to Parry the **next attack** that targets you this round.
- Each successive Parry Test is made at **Disadvantage**, reflecting mounting physical and mental strain.
- You may continue chaining Parries as long as you succeed and choose to maintain the stance.
- A failure at any point ends all defensive stances immediately.

### Counter-Attack
If you choose to take the free counter-attack on a successful Parry:

- It resolves at the **Speed of the attack you parried**.
- It does **not** consume part of your action budget.
- After the counter-attack resolves, **Parrying Stance ends**.

### Requirements to Parry
You may Parry if:
- You are wielding **any melee weapon**, including **two-handed** weapons, or
- You are **unarmed**, with at least one arm free.

You cannot Parry if:
- Stunned, Paralyzed, Prone, Restrained, or otherwise unable to move your arms.
- Your weapon is **not drawn**.

### Facing
Parry relies on **awareness and line of sight**.

| Attack Direction       | Parry Result                            |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| From the **front arc** | Parry functions normally.               |
| From the **rear arc**  | Parry Test is made at **Disadvantage**. |

### Ending Parry
Parry ends if:
- You take the counter-attack option after a successful Parry.
- You perform a different Defensive Stance.
- Your next turn begins.
- You become Stunned, Restrained, or Disarmed.
- A Parry attempt fails.
