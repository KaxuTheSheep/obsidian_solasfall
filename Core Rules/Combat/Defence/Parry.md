**Parry** is a **defensive stance** focused on **deflecting or redirecting** an incoming [[Attacks|attack]] with precise timing. A successful Parry **negates the [[Attacks|attack]] entirely**, preventing all [[Damage Types|damage]] and effects — but **failure is punished**, as a mistimed deflection exposes you to a worse hit.

## Declaring Parry
- **Parry is declared during the Declaration Phase.**
- You enter a **Parrying Stance** which remains active until your next turn, unless ended early.
- Parry does **not** occur immediately.  
    It only triggers when **the first [[Attacks|attack]] this [[Rounds|round]]** targets you.

## Parry [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]]
When the first [[Attacks|attack]] is made against you:
- Parry resolves at the **[[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] of the weapon (or unarmed strike)** being used to parry.
- If **your Parry [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] is lower** than the attacker’s [[Attacks|attack]] [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]], the [[Attacks|attack]] lands **before** your Parry completes → Parry gains **Disadvantage**.
If you are able to meet or exceed their [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] → proceed to the Parry Test.

## Parry Test (Timing Contest)
Make a **Grace Test**.
- **DN = the attacker’s [[Attacks|Attack]] Test result** (their roll total).
**Outcomes:**

| Result               | Effect                                                                                                                  |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Success**          | The [[Attacks\|attack]] is **negated** — you take **no [[Damage Types\|damage]]** and suffer **no effects**.                                           |
| **Critical Success** | The [[Attacks\|attack]] is negated, and you may make **one free [[Attacks\|attack]]** against the attacker at the [[Speed & Resolution\|speed]] of the [[Attacks\|attack]] you choose. |
| **Failure**          | You take **full [[Damage Types\|damage]]**, and the [[Attacks\|attack]]'s [[Damage Types\|damage]] die is **stepped up by one** (d6 → d8 → d10 → d12).                   |
Parry only applies to **one [[Attacks|attack]] per [[Rounds|round]]**.

## Requirements to Parry
You may Parry if:
- You are wielding **any melee weapon**, including **two-handed** [[weapons]]  
    or
- You are **unarmed**, with at least one arm free to perform the deflection.
You cannot Parry if:
- Stunned, Paralyzed, Prone, Restrained, or otherwise unable to move your arms.
- Your weapon is **not drawn**.

## [[Facing & Vision|Facing]]
Parry relies on **awareness and line of sight**.

|[[Attacks\|Attack]] Direction|Parry Result|
|---|---|
|From the **front arc**|Parry functions normally.|
|From the **rear arc**|Your Parry Test is made at **Disadvantage**.|
If using **Defensive Footwork / [[Facing & Vision|Facing]] rules**, turning to face during resolution consumes your **[[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] position**, not declaration.

## Ending Parry
Parry ends if:
- You perform a different **Defensive Stance** (e.g., switch to [[Block]]).
- Your next turn begins.
- You become Stunned / Restrained / Disarmed.
