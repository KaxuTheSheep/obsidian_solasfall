In Solasfall, creatures and characters may have access to various types of movement. Each type represents a different mode of travel, with its own mechanical and narrative implications, if a character doesn’t have a value in any of these movement types, they can’t perform that movement types.
- **Walk:** The standard movement on flat or gently uneven ground. Most creatures have a walking distance which defines how far they can move per Round under normal Conditions.
- **Run:** An exerted burst of distance that costs a full action. Your running distance is specific to your race or character and may vary. If you have already moved by walking during your turn, subtract the number of meters walked from your total running distance for that action.
- **Climb:** Movement on vertical or difficult surfaces. Creatures with climbing ability can ascend or descend walls, trees, or cliffs.
- **Swim:** Movement through water. Swimming distance is usually slower than walking.
- **Burrow:** Movement through loose earth or similar materials by digging or tunnelling. Creatures with burrow distance create tunnels that can remain passable for others unless collapsed or blocked.
- **Fly:** Movement through the air. Flight distances vary, and flight may have special Conditions such as requiring hover or avoiding obstacles.
- **Crawl:** Slow movement while low to the ground or through tight spaces. Crawl distance is generally slower than walking and may be used for stealth or to navigate confined areas.
- **Jump:** A specialized movement used to cross gaps or obstacles. It costs a half action to perform and covers a distance based on your race’s base jump bonus. If you have moved at least 3 meters by walking or running immediately before the jump, you add your Might attribute modifier (in meters) to the jump distance. Jumping may require a skill check depending on the situation.

## Using Movement
- Movement can be taken before, after, or in-between your full and half Actions each Round, allowing flexible tactics.
- The total movement used in a round cannot exceed the sum of your available movement types and their distance.
- Movement is measured in meters.
- Interruptions during climbing or other risky movement may require checks to maintain grip or balance. For example, if struck by an enemy while climbing, the GM may call for an Athletics check to avoid falling.
- Falling consequences, if any, should be handled narratively or with simple damage/fall rules.
- Different movement types may be combined within a single turn, respecting the individual distance and Actions.

## Speed of Movement
The Speed for movement types represents how quickly a creature can initiate that mode of movement from a standstill. It measures the reaction time and physical effort required to start moving, including any preparation like launching into flight or gaining footing for a climb. This speed value is crucial in combat for determining whether a character or creature can successfully move out of harm’s way before an incoming attack lands. When multiple Actions happen simultaneously, the movement speed statistic sets the priority of who moves first, reflecting how fast they can react and begin their motion. Importantly, once movement has started, the speed value does not dictate how far or fast the creature can travel over the round—only the initial start timing is affected. Different movement types have distinct speed statistics because some Actions, such as burrowing or swimming, naturally require more time and effort to begin than walking or running.
