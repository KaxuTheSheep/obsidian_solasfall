Magic is the practice of harnessing [[mana]] — a substance of countless particles that flows through all living things, the land, and the air. [[Mana]] acts as a catalyst, accelerating natural processes. Fire mages burn [[mana]] as fuel to accelerate oxidation into combustion. [[Lifebinders Institution|Lifebinders]] use [[mana]] to accelerate cellular growth into healing. The magic is not conjured from nothing — it is the world's own processes, forced to happen faster.
Only trained individuals or divine beings can harness [[mana]] effectively. Untrained exposure to [[mana]] flow produces random, unpredictable catalytic effects.
## The Soul as Chamber
Mages channel [[mana]] by using their soul as a chamber — a pipe through which [[mana]] is forced and shaped into a spell. The soul does not store [[mana]]. It conducts it. [[Mana]] passes through the caster during casting and dissipates after.

This means a mage can theoretically cast indefinitely. There is no [[mana]] pool to deplete. The limiting factor is the soul's ability to handle the flow. Forcing too much [[mana]] through too quickly causes [[Mana Overload Syndrome]] — a catastrophic magical backlash that can permanently sever a mage's connection to [[mana]] and cause lasting mental or physical [[Damage Types|damage]].

# The Three Casting Methods
Magic is expressed through three distinct casting methods. All use the same underlying [[mana]] system, but differ in _when structure is applied and how control is enforced_.
## Flux Casting (Live Casting)
Flux Casting is the real-time shaping of [[mana]] as it passes through the caster.

It is immediate, adaptive, and highly flexible, but inherently unstable due to the lack of a pre-defined structure.
### Core Properties
- Fastest decision-to-effect casting method
- Highest flexibility and improvisation
- Highest variance in outcome
- No preparation required
- Most sensitive to control errors

### Mechanical Identity

Flux Casting uses:

- Control DN (stability threshold)
- Manifest DN (full resolution threshold)
- Attribute-based shaping (Insight, Resolve, Presence)
- Enhancement scaling (increases power and instability)

### Key Principle

Flux Casting requires the caster to **form, stabilise, and release mana in real time**.

Small control deviations can cause major outcome shifts.

---

## Weave Casting (Structured Magic)
Weave Casting uses a constructed magical framework (circle, rune, or geometric sigil) that constrains [[mana]] into a predefined pattern.
Unlike Flux Casting, the spell’s structure exists before or during activation, not during freeform shaping.
Weave Casting trades flexibility for consistency and control.
### Core Properties
- Medium casting speed (varies depending on construction)
- Moderate stability
- Requires physical or inscribed structure
- Can be interrupted during construction (if mid-combat)
- More consistent than Flux Casting, less flexible than it
## Subtype: Woven Casting
Woven Casting is a fully prepared magical structure that exists before activation.
It represents the most stable form of structured magic.
> You prepare the spell beforehand and allow [[mana]] to flow into the circle to activate it.
### Core Properties
- Fastest activation of structured magic
- Highest consistency and reliability
- Requires preparation time outside of combat
- Minimal real-time control required
- Output is highly deterministic
## Subtype: Drawn Weave Casting
Drawn Weave Casting is a mid-combat construction of a magical structure.
The caster actively inscribes or forms the structure while under pressure.
### Core Properties
- Slower than Flux Casting, faster than full preparation methods
- Requires uninterrupted or semi-stable casting conditions
- Moderate flexibility during construction
- Less consistent than Woven Casting
- Structure must be completed before full effect resolves

## Key Principle

Weave Casting constrains [[mana]] through structure.  
woven Casting executes a prepared structure.  
Drawn Weave Casting constructs structure under pressure.

# Unified Casting Logic

All magic follows one core principle:

> Magic is the acceleration of natural processes through structured [[mana]] flow.

The difference between casting methods is not what magic does, but:

> when structure is imposed on mana during the casting process.

# Radiant and Umbral Magic
Radiant and Umbral [[Damage Types|damage]] types cannot be produced by trained mages regardless of [[Skills|skill]]. These forces originate from deities — [[mana]] constructs sustained by collective belief — and can only be channelled through Followers. No amount of [[Attributes|Insight]], [[Attributes|Resolve]], or [[Attributes|Presence]] grants a mage access to these types.
