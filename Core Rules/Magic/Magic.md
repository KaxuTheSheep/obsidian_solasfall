Magic is the practice of harnessing [[mana]] — a substance of countless particles that flows through all living things, the land, and the air. [[Mana]] acts as a catalyst, accelerating natural processes. Fire mages burn [[mana]] as fuel to accelerate oxidation into combustion. [[Lifebinders Institution|Lifebinders]] use [[mana]] to accelerate cellular growth into healing. The magic is not conjured from nothing — it is the world's own processes, forced to happen faster.
Only trained individuals or divine beings can harness [[mana]] effectively. Untrained exposure to [[mana]] flow produces random, unpredictable catalytic effects.

## The Soul as Chamber
Mages channel [[mana]] by using their soul as a chamber — a pipe through which [[mana]] is forced and shaped into a spell. The soul does not store [[mana]]. It conducts it. [[Mana]] passes through the caster during casting and dissipates after.

This means a mage can theoretically cast indefinitely. There is no [[mana]] pool to deplete. The limiting factor is the soul's ability to handle the flow. Forcing too much [[mana]] through too quickly causes [[Mana Overload Syndrome]] — a catastrophic magical backlash that can permanently sever a mage's connection to [[mana]] and cause lasting mental or physical [[Damage Types|damage]].

## Non-Deterministic Magic
Magic is chaotic in the mathematical sense. Small variations in control produce wildly different outputs. A highly skilled mage produces consistent results because their precision minimises variation. A novice produces unpredictable results because tiny errors cascade into something unintended.

This means magic never fully fails silently. An uncontrolled spell does not fizzle — it misfires outward, producing something real and potentially dangerous.

## Casting a Spell
Every spell has a **Base DN** split into two values:
- **Control DN** — the minimum threshold to maintain meaningful control over the spell.
- **Manifest DN** — the threshold required to cast the spell as fully intended.

To cast, roll **2d10 + chosen Mental [[Attributes|Attribute]]** and compare to both DNs.

|Result|Outcome|
|---|---|
|Meets or exceeds Manifest DN|Full success. Spell resolves as intended.|
|Meets Control DN but not Manifest|Partial. Spell fires but incompletely or unpredictably.|
|Below Control DN|Backlash. Spell misfires outward with dangerous unintended effects.|

## Mental [[Attributes]]
When casting, choose one Mental [[Attributes|Attribute]]. Only the chosen [[Attributes|attribute]] applies for that cast.

| [[Attributes\|Attribute]] | Effect                                                                                                                                               |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[Attributes\|Insight]]   | Determines how many enhancement points you control precisely. Points beyond your [[Attributes\|Insight]] score randomise.                                            |
| [[Attributes\|Resolve]]   | Reduces both DNs after points are applied. Every 1 [[Attributes\|Resolve]] reduces Control DN by 1 and Manifest DN by 1.                                             |
| [[Attributes\|Presence]]  | Amplifies specific spell effects after resolution at no additional cost. Each spell defines which of its effects [[Attributes\|Presence]] amplifies and by how much. |

## Enhancement Points
Each spell has a set of variables that can be enhanced beyond their base values by spending points. Points define precision and power — more points means a more defined, potent spell.

- Every point spent raises **Control DN by 1** and **Manifest DN by 2**.
- Points spent beyond your [[Attributes|Insight]] score produce **randomised variables**, handled by the system.
- There is no maximum points you can spend. Higher ambition means higher DNs and greater risk.

## [[Sanity]] Cost
Casting always costs [[Sanity]], reflecting the mental strain of forcing [[mana]] through the soul.

|Outcome|[[Sanity]] Cost|
|---|---|
|Full success|Base cost + 1 per point spent|
|Partial|Half of full success cost|
|Backlash|Double full success cost|

Each spell lists its own base [[Sanity]] cost.

## Backlash
When a cast falls below the Control DN, the spell misfires. The [[mana]] forced through the soul escapes outward in an uncontrolled burst. The effect is always related to the spell's nature but unpredictable in form. Each spell lists examples of possible backlash effects.

## Radiant and Umbral Magic
Radiant and Umbral [[Damage Types|damage]] types cannot be produced by trained mages regardless of [[Skills|skill]]. These forces originate from deities — [[mana]] constructs sustained by collective belief — and can only be channelled through Followers. No amount of [[Attributes|Insight]], [[Attributes|Resolve]], or [[Attributes|Presence]] grants a mage access to these types.

## Magic Circles and Runes
Magic does not require a soul to manifest. Correctly inscribed geometric structures form a physical path that [[mana]] flows through naturally, producing consistent effects without conscious control.

- Runes and circles produce the same effect every time as long as the inscription is intact.
- Variance comes only from [[mana]] supply — too little produces no output, too much risks rupture.
- Anyone can channel [[mana]] into a circle, not only mages. Magic items form similar pathways, [[Drawing & Stowing|drawing]] [[mana]] passively.
- The chaos principle does not apply to circles and runes. The geometry constrains the output.
