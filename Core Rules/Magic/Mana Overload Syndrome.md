[[Mana]] Overload Syndrome occurs when a soul is subjected to [[mana]] flow so catastrophic it shatters entirely. Unlike backlash — where [[mana]] escapes outward from an imprecise cast — [[Mana]] Overload is the soul itself failing to contain the flow at all. The pipe does not leak. It breaks.
The soul functions like glass. Hard, capable of tremendous pressure, but brittle under the wrong kind of force. There are no partial fractures. No degrees of [[Damage Types|damage]]. A soul under [[mana]] overload either survives intact or does not survive at all.

## Cause
[[Mana]] Overload cannot occur through normal casting regardless of how many enhancement points are spent or how badly a cast fails. Backlash, Exhaustion, and [[Sanity]] [[Damage Types|damage]] are the consequences of ordinary magical failure.

[[Mana]] Overload requires extraordinary [[mana]] flow — the kind generated by massive magical undertakings far beyond individual spellcasting. The creation of the [[Veil]], catastrophic ritual failure, or deliberate self-sacrifice to channel power on a civilisational scale are the [[conditions]] under which it occurs.

## Consequences
When a soul shatters, the consequences are permanent and total.
- The mage loses all ability to channel [[mana]]. No casting of any kind is possible.
- The mage loses all [[Skills|perception]] of [[mana]]. The sense through which mages experience the world's underlying flow is extinguished entirely. Not diminished — gone.
- Depending on the scale of the event, lasting physical and mental [[Damage Types|damage]] accompanies the shattering. In extreme cases the mage is reduced to a vegetative state, alive but hollowed, capable of neither thought nor [[Actions|action]] without external direction.

There is no recovery. The glass does not mend.

## The King
The most prominent known case of [[Mana]] Overload Syndrome is the current King of [[Solasfall]], who suffered a catastrophic soul shattering during the final sealing ritual of the [[Veil]]. He survives as a hollow figure — alive in body but absent in mind, incapable of governance or independent function.
His [[Conditions|condition]] is officially described as a severe illness. He rarely appears in public. When he does, Gloomshaper illusion [[magic]] is used to present a convincing [[Skills|performance]] of health and [[Attributes|presence]]. In reality the Royal Advisors govern [[Solasfall]] entirely in his name, maintaining the fiction of legitimate royal rule for political stability.
Few outside the innermost circle of power know the truth.
