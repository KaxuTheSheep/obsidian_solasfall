### Reach
Weapons with **Reach** threaten space **beyond normal melee range**.
When a creature **enters your melee threat range**, you may make **one melee attack** against them at the **expense of your Reaction**. This is called a **Reach Punish**.
- The Punish resolves at the **same Speed** the creature moves into range.
- The defender uses the **Defensive Stances** they declared during action declaration. 
    You may only perform **one Reach Punish per round**, even if multiple creatures enter your range.
**Close-In Penalty:**  
If an enemy is **within your Close-Quarters range** (i.e., they are inside _your_ Natural Range):
- Your **melee attack Tests** with this weapon are at **Disadvantage**
- Your **Parry Tests** with this weapon are at **Disadvantage**
This penalty applies **only** while the enemy remains in your Close-Quarters distance.

### Guarded(+N)
The weapon’s hilt geometry, balance, and hand protection make it **exceptional for defensive redirection**.  
When you make a **Parry**, add **+N** to your **Parry Test** result.  
This affects only **Parry**, not Attacks or Block.

### Unbalanced
Some Weapons are **difficult to control defensively** due to forward weight, uneven mass distribution, or recovery lag when deflecting force. They can hit hard, but are poor at redirecting incoming Attacks.  
When performing a **Parry** with an Unbalanced weapon:  
You suffer **–1** on your **Parry Test**.  
This applies only to **Parry**, not to **Block** or attack rolls.

### Hooking
Used to **catch**, **drag**, or **bind** an opponent’s guard.  
When you **hit** a target who is currently **Blocking**, the target must make a **Might Test** with **DN = 10 + your Might Score**.
- **Failure:** Their **Block immediately ends**.
- **Success:** Block continues normally.

### Sundering
Built to **deform plates, sheer links, or crack rigid structures**.  
When you deal damage to a target wearing Armour, Choose to Sunder before rolling damage. If any portion of the post-Block damage is applied to Armour DR, reduce the target’s Armour DR by 2 (until repaired), even if the Armour reduces the final damage to 0. If the attack is Parried, or Block prevents any damage from reaching Armour DR, Sundering does not apply:
- Deal **$\frac{3}{4}$ damage** (round down), and
- Reduce the target’s **Armour DR by 2** until repaired.  
    This choice is **situational** and rewards attrition strategies.

### Momentum
Thrives on **committed, powerful swings**.  
If you make a **Heavy Attack** with this weapon:
- **Roll one additional damage die** of the weapon’s type (Heavy Attack damage + 1 more identical die).
- On the **next round**, your **first action** has **–3 to its Speed**
Example:  
A **1d8** Momentum weapon using Heavy Attack → **2d8** damage

### Crushing
This weapon delivers force that can **break guard on impact**.
When you hit a target who is currently **Blocking**, roll **1d6**.
Reduce the target’s **Block DR** by the amount rolled **immediately**, for the **remainder of this Round**.
This reduction applies **before** damage is resolved.
If the Block Value is reduced to **0**, the defender’s **Block ends immediately**.

### Close-Quarters Proficiency
This weapon is effective **inside the guard**, where space is tight and leverage matters.
If a creature is **within your Close-Quarters range** (inside your Natural Range):
- You gain **Advantage** on melee attack Tests made with this weapon.
This applies regardless of whether the opponent wields a **Reach** weapon or not.

### Concealable
The weapon is easy to hide on the body.
- Creatures attempting to **detect if you are armed** have **Disadvantage** on relevant **Perception** checks.
- Drawing this weapon while concealed **does not automatically reveal hostility** (GM decides when tension breaks).

### Penetrating
This weapon is designed to slip into **gaps, seams, or weak points** rather than break through armour or muscle.
When an attack with this weapon **reduces a creature’s Sturdiness**,  
**also apply 1 direct Blood damage** to the target.
This direct Blood damage **ignores Sturdiness and Armour DR.**
This effect **cannot reduce a creature below 1 Blood** on its own.
**Applicability:**  
Penetrating applies only to creatures with **discernible joints or layered protection** (GM determines applicability).

### Intimidating
The weapon’s appearance or reputation provokes caution.
- When you **brandish or ready** this weapon, you gain **Advantage** on **Intimidation** Tests.
- Has **no effect** on creatures immune to fear or without emotional reasoning.

### Silent
The weapon does not produce audible noise when striking flesh or moving through air.
- **Melee Attacks** with this weapon do **not** produce detectable combat noise unless they strike Armour or metal.
- Useful for ambush and Hollowdeep stealth operations.

### Reliable
The weapon’s balance and design make it consistent and trustworthy.
- **Once per round**, you may **reroll** a **damage die** showing **1**, taking the new result.

### Nimble
This weapon can be drawn or stowed as a **free action** once per round.

### Cumbersome
Drawing or stowing this weapon requires a **full action**.

# Modifiers
### One-Handed
- Can be wielded with one hand.
- May be paired with a **shield**, a **second weapon**, or left-hand **grappling**.
- Can be switched between hands as a **free action**.

### Two-Handed
- Designed to be used with **both hands** to maintain leverage, stability, and edge alignment.
- The wielder may **momentarily release one hand** to perform **Parry**, **Grapple**, or **minor interactions** **without changing stance**.  #Wip 
  The weapon is still considered **Two-Handed** for all mechanical purposes.

### Versatile
- Can be wielded with **one** or **two** hands.
- Gains **+1 Step to Damage Die** when wielded with **two hands**  
  (example: d6 → d8 → d10, depending on the weapon’s listed die progression).

## Heavy
- Requires committed body movement to use effectively.
- Cannot benefit from **Dodge’s free 2m reposition step** (you may still Dodge, but you do not gain the step).

### Standard
- No effect
### Light
- Easy to manoeuvre in tight space.
- Can be used while **Dodge** is active without penalty.
- Can be **dual-wielded** later (once dual-wielding rules exist).
