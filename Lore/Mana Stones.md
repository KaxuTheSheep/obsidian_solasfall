**Description:**  
Mana Stones are condensed deposits of Mana, naturally occurring in the Hollowdeep or artificially created through magical processes. They store raw magical energy in a solid, portable form, allowing mages and magical tools to tap into it when direct access to ambient Mana is insufficient or impractical.

### Properties:
- **Purity & Stability:** More purified Mana Stones contain a higher concentration of Mana per unit mass, but are less stable and can release energy unpredictably if mishandled. Larger stones tend to hold more Mana.
- **Colour Variations:** Impurities in the stone cause colour differences — e.g., copper yields greenish stones, magnesium yields whiter stones. These colours have no inherent magical property but can indicate composition.
- **Usage:** Primarily used as magical batteries for tools, artefacts, and powerful spells. Most mages draw Mana directly from the environment, using stones only for complex or high-demand spells.
- **Currency Connection:** Historically, Mana Stones were used as currency, measured via a Mana Scale. Modern trade converts standardized stones into Gilded Mints via The Ministry of Balance, particularly for The Explorers' Guild's expeditions.

### Handling & Risks:  
Mana Stones themselves pose no inherent danger, but improper handling can cause Mana Overload Syndrome, a severe and potentially fatal release of concentrated Mana.
