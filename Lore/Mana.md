A fundamental particle of creation — the raw substance from which Magic draws its power.

## Nature
Mana is neither purely physical nor spiritual, but a liminal energy existing between the material and metaphysical. It flows through all living things, the land, and the air. To the untrained it is nothing at all — imperceptible, intangible, absent. To mages, it is unmistakable.

Mana is not wielded from outside. It passes through a caster's soul during casting and dissipates after. The soul acts as a chamber — a pipe that shapes mana into intent. Nothing is stored. Everything flows.

## Function
Mana acts as a catalyst, accelerating natural processes rather than conjuring effects from nothing. Fire mages burn mana to accelerate oxidation into combustion. Lifebinders use mana to accelerate cellular growth into healing. The magic is not created — it is the world's own processes, forced to happen faster.

When concentrated, mana forms **Mana Stones** — stable crystalline vessels capable of storing and releasing mana in measured amounts. All major magical infrastructure, including the Veil and the Rift to the Hollowdeep, depends on refined mana or Mana Stones for operation.

## The Fallows
Not all mana is neutral. Mana particles that have passed through or near the origin Rift carry a specific encoding — time acceleration. When this corrupted mana saturates an area it causes the Fallows, accelerating decay, growth, and entropy simultaneously. The world does not rot in the Fallows. It rushes forward until nothing remains.

This encoding is invisible and undetectable to most. Even trained mages cannot distinguish corrupted mana from clean mana without specialised knowledge.

## Deities and Mana
Deities are not beings who harness mana — they are mana constructs, shaped and sustained by the collective belief of their followers. Worship is not metaphor. It is the literal mechanism that maintains a deity's existence as a coherent mana structure.

Followers do not surrender their souls to their deity. Instead their soul synchronises directly with the deity's mana construct, forming a persistent connection. Divine power flows through this connection rather than from the Follower's own casting. The soul remains the Follower's own — the deity is not a replacement but a signal, running alongside the soul's natural function.

## Sources
Mana is naturally generated through the **World's Breath** — an invisible cycle that renews ambient mana over centuries. It is found in abundance within the Hollowdeep, where unstable realms and collapsed realities compress raw mana into mineral form.

## Perception
Mana has no visible form, texture, or sound. Its presence is felt, not seen:

- A pressure in the air, like a storm about to break.
- A tug beneath the skin, as though the body remembers something ancient.
- A vibration of thought, faint but alive, threading through the fabric of the world.

Those who suffer complete soul severance — such as through Mana Overload — lose this perception entirely. The world becomes quiet in a way that cannot be described to those who never felt it.
