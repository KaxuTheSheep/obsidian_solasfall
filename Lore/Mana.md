A fundamental particle of creation — the raw substance from which Magic draws its power.

## Nature
Mana exists at a quantum level — particles too small to observe directly, yet present in everything. They flow through living things, the land, the air, and the space between worlds. To the untrained, mana is imperceptible. To mages, it is unmistakable — felt rather than seen, a persistent sixth sense that never fully quiets.

Mana particles naturally form **currents and circuits** through the world, following paths shaped by the environment around them. These currents are not coordinated. A single mana particle accelerating growth in one root of a plant may be countered by another decelerating it a centimetre away. At this scale the effects cancel out, and the world behaves as though mana were not present at all. Only when mana is coordinated — deliberately directed by a mage, or concentrated by circumstance — do its effects become meaningful.

## Excitation
Every mana particle exists somewhere on a spectrum between two extremes, measured by its **excitation state**.

- **Inert mana** sits at equilibrium — neither accelerating nor decelerating phenomena. Left alone, all mana naturally seeks this state.
- **Vivant excitation** pushes mana away from equilibrium in one direction, causing it to accelerate natural processes. Fire, growth, oxidation, cellular repair — Vivant mana forces these to happen faster.
- **Mordant excitation** pushes mana in the opposite direction, causing it to decelerate natural processes. Decay slows. Movement resists. Time, in sufficient concentration, crawls.

Excitation is not binary. Particles exist across a range of intensity within each state, from faint traces barely distinguishable from inert to extreme concentrations that produce dramatic and dangerous effects. This variance is what gives mana its complexity — and what makes the difference between a trained mage and an untrained one.

Mana can also interact with physical matter directly, exerting pressure against particles and molecules. This is the basis of wind magic and force effects — not a separate property, but Vivant mana accelerating the movement of air or matter in a directed way.

## The World's Breath
Mana does not originate in Solasfall's world. It flows in from outside — a cosmic current that permeates all dimensions and worlds simultaneously. This is the **World's Breath**: an invisible, universal tide that pushes mana particles away from their natural inert state and into excitation.

Without the World's Breath, all mana would eventually neutralise. With it, the world is in constant low-level flux — mana being pushed toward Vivant and Mordant states, then slowly drifting back toward inert, then pushed again. This cycle sustains the ambient magical environment of every world the Breath touches.

The World's Breath does not discriminate between worlds. It flows equally through Solasfall, through the Hollowdeep's web, and through whatever lies beyond the rift connected to the Fallows. Each world expresses it differently depending on local conditions, but the source is universal.

## The Soul as Conductor
Mages do not generate mana. They conduct it.

Mana flows through a mage's soul during casting, and the soul shapes which particles are selected, directed, and released. The soul acts as a sorting mechanism — drawing in mana from the ambient environment, imposing direction and coordination on what would otherwise be random, and releasing it toward an intended effect. Nothing is stored. Everything flows through and dissipates after.

This means a mage can theoretically cast indefinitely. The limiting factor is not supply but the soul's capacity to handle the flow. Forcing too much mana through the soul too quickly causes **Mana Overload Syndrome** — a catastrophic backlash that can permanently damage or sever the soul's conducting ability, leaving the mage unable to feel or direct mana at all.

## Deities and Mana
Deities are not beings who harness mana — they are mana constructs, shaped and sustained by the collective belief of their followers. Worship is not metaphor. It is the literal mechanism that maintains a deity's existence as a coherent mana structure.

Followers do not surrender their souls to their deity. Instead, their soul synchronises directly with the deity's mana construct, forming a persistent connection. Divine power flows through this connection rather than from the Follower's own conducting. The soul remains the Follower's own — the deity is not a replacement but a signal, running alongside the soul's natural function.

## Perception
Mana has no visible form, texture, or sound. Its presence is felt, not seen.

Trained mages perceive mana continuously — a passive awareness that exists alongside ordinary senses, like a permanent pressure in the background of thought. Currents, concentrations, and excitation states become readable with experience.

The untrained cannot perceive ambient mana under normal conditions. However, when mana differentials are extreme — such as crossing through a Rift into the Hollowdeep — even untrained individuals feel something. A sudden heaviness, a shift in the air that has no physical cause, a sensation of crossing a threshold. This fades as the body adjusts, and does not return unless the differential becomes extreme again.

Those who suffer complete soul severance through Mana Overload lose mage perception entirely. The world becomes quiet in a way that cannot be described to those who never felt it.
