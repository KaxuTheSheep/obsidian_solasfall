**Overview**  
The Veil is an immense magical barrier encasing Solasfall, separating it from the encroaching corruption of the Fallows. Though it appears to the public as a tranquil sky with sun, clouds, and stars, it is in truth a dense, mana-fuelled illusion — a wall of thick, radiant mist that holds back the world’s decay.

### Origin
The Veil was conceived during the reign of the current King, in collaboration with The Royal Mages Its creation marked one of the greatest magical undertakings in recorded history — and one of its darkest. During the final sealing ritual, the King suffered from **Mana Overload Syndrome**, a catastrophic magical backlash that severed his connection to the Aether and left him mentally fractured. The Veil’s success came at the cost of his mind and the permanent binding of hundreds of mages’ souls to its structure.

### Function & Maintenance
- The Veil acts as a **containment and camouflage field**, halting the advance of the Fallows and maintaining an illusion of normality within the city.
- It simulates a full **day–night cycle**, complete with artificial light, clouds, and rainfall. This illusion extends beyond the Outer Walls, sustaining farms and rivers that would otherwise wither.
- The barrier’s stability demands vast amounts of mana, replenished through **Mana Stones** harvested from the Hollowdeep.
- The Royal Mages manage the Veil through continuous rituals, overseen by the Royal Advisors, who often exploit its control for personal or political gain — manipulating weather or sunlight for events, appearances, or to symbolically bless decrees.

### Appearance
From within Solasfall, the Veil appears as a natural sky — serene and real. Yet, at the city’s fringes, the illusion falters. Those who venture too near its edge find the “sky” turning pale and clouded, until the world becomes an endless wall of mist. Approaching the Veil too closely can induce vertigo, hallucinations, and even brief glimpses of what lies beyond: the bleeding hues of the Fallows.

### Instability & the Rift
Every four weeks, when the Rift to the Hollowdeep is opened, the Veil weakens temporarily. During this time, faint distortions appear overhead — flashes of crimson light, echoes of distorted landscapes, and for some, whispered voices. Though officially dismissed as “mana storms,” many fear these moments as signs that the Fallows are slowly winning.

### Maintenance & Rituals
- The Veil requires constant sustenance through **Mana Stones** harvested from the Hollowdeep.
- Regular maintenance rituals are performed by The Royal Mages at the Veil’s anchor points — towering pylons embedded with runic foci that draw upon stored mana.
- Large-scale renewals occur every **four weeks**, aligned with the Rift Cycle. These ceremonies are citywide events, drawing both reverence and unease from the populace.
- The immense upkeep costs are managed and enforced by The Ministry of Balance, which levies steep **taxation and resource tithes** across Solasfall to fund the Veil’s maintenance.
- This economic burden fuels growing dissent among the poorer districts and the rebels, who question whether the Veil’s protection justifies the price.

### Public Perception
To most citizens, the Veil is a miracle — the great shield that allows civilisation to exist. Children are raised under its painted stars; poets praise its “ever-living dawn.” Yet among the lower classes, resentment brews: the **mana tithes** demanded for its maintenance are steep, and rumours claim the Veil feeds not only on mana but on souls.  
The Rebel Group is divided — some seek to tear it down, believing freedom lies beyond; others fear doing so would doom the last city in the world.
