The Veil is an immense magical structure encasing Solasfall, separating it from the accelerated decay of the world beyond its boundaries. To most citizens it appears as a natural sky — sun, clouds, stars, and weather cycling in familiar rhythms. In truth it is two distinct systems operating in concert: a barrier that keeps time inside Solasfall from racing toward ruin, and a controlled illusion layer that the Royal Mages use to simulate and manipulate the sky above the city.

## The Time Problem
The world outside Solasfall is not simply dangerous — it is dying at speed. Through the same rift phenomenon that connects worlds, Solasfall's world is in equilibrium contact with a realm where time moves at a dramatically accelerated rate. Everything outside the Veil experiences this — decay, growth, and entropy rushing forward simultaneously. Those who step beyond the boundary do not perceive their own acceleration. Their minds experience time normally while their bodies age and deteriorate in moments.

The Veil's primary function is to suppress this effect inside the city. It achieves this through an enormous sustained field of Mordant-excited mana, decelerating the equilibrium pull from the fast-time connection and maintaining something close to normal temporal conditions within Solasfall's boundaries. Without it, the city would gradually synchronise with the world outside.

## Construction & The Bound Souls
The Veil was conceived and executed during the reign of the current King, in collaboration with the Royal Mages. It stands as the greatest magical undertaking in recorded history — and one of its darkest.

Mana at the scale required for the Veil cannot simply be directed by living mages. The currents involved are too vast and too constant to be held in shape by conductors working in shifts. The solution, devised at enormous cost, was to permanently bind hundreds of mages' souls into the Veil's structure. These souls do not power the Veil — they serve as **permanent current guidelines**, shaping the mana circuits through the barrier the way a riverbed shapes water. They provide the structure that raw mana flow cannot maintain on its own.

The King himself participated in the final sealing ritual. The scale of mana conducted through a single soul at that moment caused catastrophic **Mana Overload Syndrome**, permanently severing his connection to mana and leaving him mentally fractured. The Veil held. The King did not.

This sacrifice is official history in Solasfall, taught in institutions and commemorated in the renewal ceremonies. What is less publicised is the nature of the bound souls — whether they retain any awareness, whether they experience the passage of time, and whether the Veil's eventual failure would release or simply extinguish them.

## The Barrier
The Veil's barrier function operates through a sustained Mordant mana field maintained by the bound soul circuits and replenished continuously through **Mana Stones** — predominantly Mordant Stones harvested from the Hollowdeep.

The boundary itself arises from the differential between the Veil's interior and the world outside. Inside, mana is partially isolated from the World's Breath, slowly neutralising toward inert without continuous replenishment. Outside, the World's Breath pushes mana to extremes unimpeded. The sharp boundary between these two states creates natural resistance — not a wall, but a pressure gradient that becomes increasingly difficult to cross the closer one gets to the Veil's edge.

From within the city, this is invisible. At the fringes, the illusion layer thins and the mana density becomes perceptible even to the untrained — a heaviness in the air, a wrongness in the light. Those who press further find the boundary mist-like and total, and beyond it, brief glimpses of the Fallows: the bleeding hues of a world that has already run too far forward.

## The Sky Layer
Separate from the barrier, the Royal Mages maintain a controlled Vivant mana field that physically manipulates light across the interior of the Veil. This is not illusion — it is literal alteration of light's properties, polarising it and adjusting wavelengths and amplitudes to produce the appearance of sun, cloud, stars, and weather.

The sky layer requires active management rather than passive maintenance. Royal Mages operate it continuously, which gives them — and through them, the Royal Advisors — direct control over the apparent conditions inside Solasfall at any given moment.

This control is exercised routinely for political and social purposes. The day-night cycle is standard. Weather patterns are adjusted for agriculture and public events. Sunrise and sunset can be delayed or advanced. A noble family hosting an event under a specific sky can petition — and pay — for those conditions to be arranged. The Royal Advisors have been known to associate particular light conditions with decrees or ceremonies, creating an implicit language of divine favour written in weather.

The sky layer also extends beyond the city's outer walls, sustaining farms and rivers that would otherwise be exposed to the unfiltered time acceleration of the outside world.

## Instability & The Rift Cycle
Every four weeks, the Royal Mages deliberately weaken the Veil to harness the Fallows-adjacent mana needed to open the Rift to the Hollowdeep. During this window the barrier's Mordant field is partially disrupted, and the influx of Vivant-excited mana from the Rift process temporarily destabilises the interior mana environment.

Citizens experience this as distortions in the sky — flickering light, brief crimson flashes, distorted reflections where the sky layer fails to compensate. Some report whispered sounds with no source. The official designation is *mana storms*, attributed to natural fluctuation. The Royal Mages know better.

Full renewal of the Veil's structure occurs on the same four-week schedule, aligned with the Rift cycle. These ceremonies are citywide events, drawing both reverence and unease from the populace. They are the moments when the Veil's fragility is most visible, and when rumours about what would happen if it failed circulate most freely.

## Maintenance & Cost
The Veil's continuous operation demands enormous quantities of Mordant Stones. The Ministry of Balance manages procurement and funding through taxation and resource tithes levied across Solasfall. This economic burden falls heaviest on the poorer districts, who pay the most proportionally and benefit least from the sky layer's political uses.

Dissent is growing. The Rebel Group is divided — some believe freedom lies beyond the Veil, not understanding or disbelieving the time acceleration that waits outside. Others know exactly what the Veil prevents, and fear that weakening it for any reason courts catastrophe.

Rumours persist among the lower districts that the Veil feeds not only on mana but on souls — that the bound mages were not volunteers, that the cost of the Veil's creation is still being paid in ways the Institutions refuse to acknowledge.

Some of those rumours are true.

## Public Perception
To most citizens the Veil is a miracle — the great shield that allows civilisation to exist. Children are raised under its painted stars. Poets praise its ever-living dawn. The renewal ceremonies are occasions of genuine reverence.

The sky layer reinforces this. A population that watches the sun rise and set on a familiar rhythm, that sees rain come when crops need it, that lives under a sky that feels *managed* rather than arbitrary — that population does not question the Veil's necessity. This is not accidental.
